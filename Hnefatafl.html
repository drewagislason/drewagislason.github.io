Hnefatafl

Hnefataflt is a game of Vikings and Kings. Similar to chess, it is a game of pure skill: no luck is involved from dice or cards.

Hnefatafl, invented by Norsement circa 400 AD, is actually older than chess. Chess, by contrast, was invented circa 600 AD in India where it was called chaturanga. Both are derived from ancient Roman board games.

Hnefatafl Rules

  1. The Board
    • The board is 11 x 11, like a chess board but bigger, squares are not alternating colors
    • The middle square and for corner square are specially marked as "king" squares
    • The may be decorated to show initial setup
  2. The Pieces
    • The game starts with 12 white (defending) Vikings and one white King
    • The game starts with 24 black (attacking) Vikings
    • White is defending: The king and his Vikings start in the middle of the board
    • Black is attacking: The raiding Vikings start at the 4 edges of the board
    • It is common to use the term pawn rather than Viking for the bulk of the pieces
  3. The Game
    • Win for white: the King to escapes to one of the 4 corners of the board
    • Win for black: the King does not escape
      • Surround the white King on all 4 sides with black pieces
      • Surround the white King on 3 sides with 4th side center square
      • Surround the white King on 3 sides at edge with NO REMAINING white Vikings
      • White has no moves left
    • There are no ties in this game: Either Black wins or White wins.
    • Black (attacker) moves first
  4. Movement
    • All pieces move like chess rooks: that is a straight line up, down, left, right
    • No piece can move diagonally
    • No piece can occupy a square occupied by another piece
    • No piece can move through another piece
    • Only the King can land on the 4 corners or center square
    • Any piece can move through the center square: only the King can land there
  5. Kills
    • A kill removes an enemy piece from the board
    • Sandwich an enemy piece with two of your pieces (attacking from in front and behind) to kill it
    • Each corner acts as an enemy piece for purposes of a kill
    • It is harmless to move between two standing enemy pieces
    • The king cannot be killed in this manner: black must surround the king to kill him

Hnefatafl Advanced Rules With Examples

  1. Flanking (Shield Wall) kills
    • This maneuver prevents 2 or more Vikings of the same color from blocking the edge of the board
    • The enemy can surround the Vikings and capture all of them with a flanking move
    • The white King cannot be killed by flanking even if all his surrounded Vikings are taken
  2. No Escape
    • If white cannot move, black wins
  3. Repetition Win
    • Like in chess, if the same moves are repeated by both players 3 times the game is over
    • Unlike in chess, there is no tie: if the King cannot escape black wins

Hnefatafl Notation

Hnefatafl uses similar notation as chess, for both FEN (board layout) and algebraic (moves).

To orient yourself, if you've never seen notated chess, read:

  • FEN: https://www.chess.com/terms/fen-chess
  • Algebraic: https://www.chess.com/terms/chess-notation

Hnefetafl uses an 11x11 board layout, rather than the 8x8 used by chess, so it looks a little different. And the pieces are simpler, only black and white Vikings and one white King.

Hnefetafl 11x11 Board Starting Layout With Coordinates:

11  . . . B B B B B . . .
10  . . . . . B . . . . .
 9  . . . . . . . . . . .
 8  B . . . . V . . . . B
 7  B . . . V V V . . . B
 6  B B . V V K V V . B B
 5  B . . . V V V . . . B
 4  B . . . . V . . . . B
 3  . . . . . . . . . . .
 2  . . . . . B . . . . .
 1  . . . B B B B B . . .
    a b c d e f g h i j k

HEN (Hnefatafl FEN) uses only the 1st two fields of FEN: placement and next player. The other FEN fields are specific to chess and are not relevant to Hnefatafl.

Each rank is described starting with rank 11 down to 1 with a "/" character between each rank. The blank Vikings are represented by a lowercase "v", white by a capital "V" and the white king by a captial "K". 1 or more empty squares are represented by a number, e.g. 5 is 5 blank squares.

A HEN representation of initial layout (black moves 1st) would look like:

3BBBBB3/5B5/11/B4V4B/B3VVV3B/BB1VVKVV1BB/B3VVV3B/B4V4B/11/5B5/3BBBBB3 B

As a short-hand, 3 dashes represent the starting layout placement.

---

Moves are annotated in turns similar to chess but always with full disambiguation. For example, in Hnefatafl an opening turn might be: 1. Bb6b11 Vf4c4.

If a move resulted in 1 or more kills, and x is used, just like in chess: 9. Bc2xc11.

The hashtag is used to indicate end of game (a win by either black or white). In the following example, white has won because the king has escaped to a corner: 23. Bj10j3 Kc1k1#.

After the ending hashtag and optional # of games won by each side (black-white), e.g. 0-1.

Whitespace, whether spaces, tabs, or line endings are ignored.

A .hen file contains up to 2 sections: position and moves. An example .hen file is below.
